## Design Output Risks

This skill encourages risk-aware creative practice, but it cannot guarantee that a generated concept or image is safe to publish. Before commercial use, review for:

- **Trademark risk**: confusingly similar logos, brand names, product marks, or trade dress.
- **Copyright risk**: copied artworks, characters, illustrations, photos, layouts, or distinctive expressive elements.
- **Artist-style risk**: direct imitation of a living artist or a highly recognizable signature style.
- **Publicity and privacy risk**: recognizable people, private places, or sensitive personal imagery.
- **Font and asset licensing**: unlicensed typefaces, stock images, icons, textures, mockups, or templates.
- **Cultural sensitivity**: motifs, symbols, or traditional patterns used without context or permission.
- **Production risk**: outputs that are too low-resolution, too detailed, too many colors, or unsuitable for the intended print process.

## Safe-Use Guidance

- Use movements and broad design languages as references, not exact artworks as targets.
- Replace AI-generated approximations of logos or brand marks with official vector files when brand use is authorized.
- Keep records of source assets and licenses for commercial or contest submissions.
- Ask a qualified lawyer for legal review when a design is high-value, client-facing, brand-sensitive, or likely to be manufactured at scale.
